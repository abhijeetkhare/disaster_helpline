Right to Information (RTI)

The Right to Information Act, 2005 empowers citizens to seek information from public authorities, promoting transparency and accountability. Under this Act, you have the right to request information related to the functioning, decision-making processes, and operations of public authorities.

1. What is RTI?

RTI stands for Right to Information. It is a fundamental right under the Constitution of India. The RTI Act, 2005 allows Indian citizens to request information from public authorities. The Act mandates timely response to citizen requests for government information.

2. How to File an RTI Request

To file an RTI request, follow these steps:

  • Identify the public authority that holds the information you need.
  • Prepare your RTI application in writing. Include your full name, contact details, and specific information you are seeking.
  • Submit the application along with the required application fee to the Public Information Officer (PIO) of the relevant public authority.
  • The PIO is obligated to respond within 30 days of receiving the request.
